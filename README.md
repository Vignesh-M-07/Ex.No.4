### Explanation

Digital learning platforms have transformed the way students access educational content. Online learning systems provide video lectures, assignments, assessments, and discussion forums that allow students to learn remotely at their own pace.

Artificial intelligence and structured prompts can be used to generate explanations, comparisons, and learning support related to online education systems. Different prompt types help produce responses with varying levels of detail, clarity, and perspective.

### Scenario

A university is implementing an online learning platform to support students who cannot attend traditional classroom sessions. The platform includes recorded lectures, quizzes, progress tracking, and discussion forums.

Target users include:

University students

Instructors delivering online courses

Educational institutions adopting digital learning tools

### Procedure and Prompt Examples
**1. Comparative Analysis Prompt**

This prompt type focuses on comparing two different systems or approaches.

Prompt Example

“Analyze the differences between self-paced online courses and instructor-led classroom courses based on learning flexibility, accessibility, and interaction.”

Expected Response

Self-paced online courses allow students to study according to their own schedule.

Instructor-led classroom courses require students to follow fixed lecture timings.

Online systems provide digital resources, while classroom learning relies on direct interaction with instructors.

**2. Experiential Perspective Prompt**

This prompt asks the response from a personal or professional viewpoint.

Prompt Example

“Assume you are an instructor teaching through an online learning platform. Explain how the platform helps you manage lectures, assignments, and student communication.”

Expected Response

Ability to upload lecture videos and digital study materials.

Tools for assigning quizzes and grading assignments online.

Communication through discussion forums and messages.

Easier monitoring of student progress through analytics.

**3. Everyday Functioning Prompts**

These prompts simplify complex ideas by connecting them to everyday experiences.

Prompt Example

“Explain the working of an online learning platform by relating it to everyday activities such as watching tutorials, using messaging apps, or organizing notes digitally.”

Expected Response

Watching recorded lectures is similar to viewing tutorial videos online.

Communication with instructors is like chatting in messaging apps.

Saving notes and assignments resembles storing documents in digital folders.

**4. Universal Prompt Structures**

This prompt type encourages clear and structured explanations.

Prompt Example

“Describe the process a student follows to complete an online course from enrollment to certification.”

Expected Response

The student registers on the learning platform.

The student enrolls in the chosen course.

Learning materials and video lectures are accessed.

Assignments and quizzes are submitted online.

Progress is evaluated and certification is provided after course completion.

**5. Prompt Size Limitations**

Large prompts may produce lengthy responses, so they can be divided into smaller prompts for clarity.

Large Prompt Example

“Discuss the role of online learning platforms in improving accessibility, providing interactive learning, supporting assessments, and monitoring student performance.”

Refined Smaller Prompts

“Explain how online learning platforms improve accessibility for students.”

“Describe the interactive features available in digital learning systems.”

“How do online learning platforms conduct quizzes and assignments?”

“Explain how student progress is monitored in online education platforms.”

Expected Response

Each prompt generates a focused explanation.

Smaller prompts improve clarity and make responses easier to understand.

### Result

After applying different prompt engineering techniques, it was observed that:

• Comparative prompts help evaluate different learning methods.
• Experiential prompts provide user-centered insights.
• Everyday prompts simplify technical explanations.
• Universal prompt structures create clear step-by-step responses.
• Prompt size limitations help manage complex topics effectively.

Various prompt engineering techniques were successfully applied to the online learning platform scenario. Choosing appropriate prompt structures improves the clarity and quality of AI-generated responses, which is useful for explaining modern digital education systems.
